“Deda Ena” — Georgian Sunday School in Denver, Colorado
Denver is home to a few hundreds of children of Georgian heritage, including children of recently immigrated families and those born in the United States. 
As parents help their children become engaged members of American society, they also recognize the importance of preserving their cultural roots. Maintaining a connection to Georgian traditions and, most importantly, the Georgian language helps children develop a strong multicultural identity and meaningful ties across generations and identities.
Bridging Educational Needs Through Heritage and Culture
In 2024, the Colorado Georgian Community Association Ertoba established “Deda Ena”, a Georgian Sunday School in Denver, to support families in strengthening language, cultural connection, and a sense of community among younger generations. Through language instruction and creative educational activities, students develop Georgian reading, writing, and conversational skills while learning about Georgian history, culture, and traditions.
Our curriculum is inspired by the educational philosophy of the Georgian educator Iakob Gogebashvili and his iconic textbook Deda Ena, a foundational educational resource in Georgia even in current days. We also incorporate additional materials, including 33 Keys and literary and informational texts from open sources developed with support from USAID in Tbilisi (2018–2022).
Today, our school serves twenty students in a welcoming environment where instruction is adapted to each child’s age and needs.  More than a language program, Deda Ena is a place where Georgian children build friendships, share traditions, strengthen cultural identity, and experience community. Our greatest inspiration is seeing students approach learning Georgian with enthusiasm, curiosity, and pride.
Meet Our Educator
Tamar Kochlamazashvili leads the educational program. She brings more than 25 years of experience working with children both in Georgia and in the U.S. Mrs. Tamar specializes in English Language and Literature and Early Childhood Development and has taught in schools and child development centers in Tbilisi, Georgia, and in Denver, Colorado.  Drawing on extensive classroom experience and expertise in languages, Tamar Kochlamazishvili creates meaningful educational experiences tailored to each child’s needs.
Our Supporters
We are grateful for the continued financial and technical support of The Georgian Association, a nationwide nonprofit organization supporting the Georgian American community. Since 2025, it has served as the principal donor of Georgian Sunday School in Denver, Colorado.
